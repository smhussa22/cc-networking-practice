# Behavioral

## Overview

Behavioral interview questions assess how you approach problems, collaborate with others, handle failure, and make decisions under pressure. Strong answers use the STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result) and demonstrate self-awareness, ownership, and technical depth. The goal is to prove that technical capability is paired with engineering judgment.

## Conceptual Questions

- What is the STAR format for answering behavioral questions?
- What is the difference between a technical story and a behavioral story?
- Why do interviewers ask behavioral questions at technical companies?
- What does "ownership" mean in an engineering context?
- What does it mean to have strong engineering judgment?
- How do you demonstrate that you learned from a failure?

## Technical Decision and Trade-Off Questions

- Describe a time you made the wrong architectural decision. What happened and what did you learn?
- Describe a time you chose the right tool or technology despite pushback. How did you make the case?
- Describe a time you disagreed with documentation or advice. What did you do?
- Describe a time you had to choose between two equally valid technical approaches. How did you decide?
- Describe a feature or component you cut because it was too complex. Why was that the right call?
- Describe a technical shortcut you took that you later regretted.
- Describe a time when a technical decision you made had unexpected consequences.
- What would you redesign in one of your projects today, and why?
- What assumptions did you make in a project that turned out to be wrong?
- What are the limitations of a system you built that you didn't anticipate?

## Debugging and Problem-Solving Questions

- Describe the hardest bug you ever fixed. Walk through your debugging process.
- Describe a production-like failure you simulated or reproduced. What did you learn?
- Describe a time when a problem took much longer to debug than expected.
- Describe a time when the root cause of a bug surprised you.
- Describe a time when you fixed a bug that had been open for a long time.
- Describe a concurrency issue that took a long time to diagnose.
- Describe a time you used an unconventional method to debug a problem.
- Describe a time you debugged a problem in a system you didn't build.

## Project and Ownership Questions

- Walk me through the most complex system you've built.
- What part of your projects are you most proud of?
- What took the longest to build in your projects?
- What would break first in your project at 100x scale?
- What would break first in your project at 10x users?
- How did you scope and prioritize work on an open-ended project?
- Describe a time you had to deliver something under a tight deadline.
- Describe a time you took on more responsibility than was expected of you.
- Describe a time you had to say no to a feature request.
- Describe a time you advocated for technical debt reduction.
- Describe a time you had to hand off a project you built to someone else.

## Collaboration and Communication Questions

- Describe a time you had to explain a complex technical concept to a non-technical stakeholder.
- Describe a time you had to give critical feedback on someone's code or design.
- Describe a time you received critical feedback and how you responded.
- Describe a time when a teammate's decision conflicted with yours. How did you resolve it?
- Describe a time you worked effectively with a remote or distributed team.
- Describe a time you helped onboard or mentor a junior engineer.
- Describe a time you had to work with someone who had a very different technical background.

## Learning and Growth Questions

- What is the most technically challenging thing you've ever done?
- What is the most important thing you've learned in the last year?
- How do you stay current with developments in networking and systems?
- Describe a time you had to learn a new technology quickly and apply it.
- Describe a time you were out of your depth and how you recovered.
- What technical topic do you most want to go deeper on?
- What do you wish you had known before starting your most recent project?

## Failure and Resilience Questions

- Describe a project that failed or was cancelled. What caused it?
- Describe a time you missed a deadline. What happened?
- Describe a time you shipped something that broke in production. What did you do?
- Describe a time you underestimated the complexity of a task.
- Describe a time you had to pivot your technical approach mid-project.
- Describe the biggest mistake you've made as an engineer. What did you learn?

## Engineering Judgment Questions

- When do you decide to build versus buy?
- When do you decide to refactor versus rewrite?
- When do you add a test versus trust the code?
- When do you optimize early versus wait until there is a bottleneck?
- When do you introduce abstraction versus keep it simple?
- How do you decide when a design is good enough?
- How do you evaluate whether a system is production-ready?
- How do you communicate risk to stakeholders?
- How do you decide when to ask for help?
